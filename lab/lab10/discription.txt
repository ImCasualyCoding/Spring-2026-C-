

Starts with fork() which makes a duplicate of the calling process
establishing a parent child relationship

Once the child is created the execlp() is called to replave the childs memory

Parent process utilizes the wait() system call to pause its own execution until the 
child process completes its task and terminates

This prevents the accumulation of defunct processes

Combining these calls an operating sysyem can dynamically manage multiple concurrent
tasks while maintaining a clear hierachy of control.
